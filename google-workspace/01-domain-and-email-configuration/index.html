Domain & Email Configuration

Domain verification, Gmail activation, mail routing, and email authentication setup.

Overview

This section demonstrates configuring a custom domain and securing email delivery within Google Workspace.

Domain Verification

Domain ownership was verified using a DNS CNAME record in Cloudflare, confirming control of the domain and enabling Google Workspace services.

Google Workspace verification code

CNAME record configured in Cloudflare DNS

Domain successfully verified in Admin Console

Gmail Activation

Gmail was enabled for the domain, allowing users to send and receive email through Google Workspace.

Gmail activation prompt in Admin Console

Gmail successfully activated

MX Records (Mail Routing)

MX records were configured in Cloudflare to route inbound email to Google Workspace mail servers.

MX record configured in Cloudflare

SPF (Sender Policy Framework)

An SPF record was configured to define which mail servers are authorised to send email on behalf of the domain.

Configured value:
v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all

SPF TXT record configured in Cloudflare

DKIM

DKIM signing was configured to provide cryptographic validation of outgoing email messages.

DKIM key generated in Google Admin Console

DKIM TXT record added to Cloudflare DNS

Successful DKIM authentication validation

DMARC

A DMARC policy was configured to monitor authentication results and provide reporting on potential spoofing or failures.

DMARC policy configured in DNS

Summary

  • Verified domain ownership via DNS
  • Enabled Gmail for hosted email
  • Configured MX routing for inbound mail
  • Implemented SPF, DKIM, and DMARC
These configurations provide a secure, production-ready email setup, improving deliverability and reducing spoofing risk.
