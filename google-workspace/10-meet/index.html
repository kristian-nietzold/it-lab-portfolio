# 10. Google Meet ## Overview This section demonstrates the configuration of Google Meet to provide secure, controlled, and scalable communication across the organisation. The implementation focuses on: - Secure meeting access and participant control - Host moderation and governance - Safe collaboration with internal and external users - Support for hybrid working environments --- ## Meet Safety Settings Google Meet safety settings were configured at the organisational level to ensure consistent behaviour across all meetings. ![Meet safety settings](screenshots/10-01-meet-safety-settings.png) ### Configuration - Meeting access: **Allowed (including external meetings)** - Access type: **Trusted (domain-aware access control)** - Waiting rooms: **Enabled (lobby control enforced)** - Host management: **Enabled by default** - External participant warning: **Enabled** - Incoming call restrictions: **Limited to organisation and contacts** - Screen sharing: **Enabled for all users** - Chat, Polls, Q&A: **Enabled** - Continuous meeting chat: **Enabled (host-controlled)** - Room check-in: **Enabled (for physical meeting spaces)** - Meet Media API: **Enabled (supports third-party integrations)** --- ## Design Rationale The configuration balances usability with security: - **Waiting rooms** - Prevent unauthorised access - Ensure hosts approve participants before entry - **Host management** - Gives hosts full control over meetings - Allows muting, removing users, and restricting actions - **Trusted access model** - Allows external collaboration while maintaining control - Reduces friction for legitimate business meetings - **External participant warnings** - Improves awareness of potential risk - Helps prevent accidental data exposure - **Collaboration features (chat, polls, Q&A)** - Improve meeting engagement and productivity --- ## Validation The configuration was tested to ensure: - External users are placed in waiting rooms when required - Hosts must approve participant access - External users are clearly labelled in meetings - Host controls function correctly (mute/remove/restrict actions) - Meeting features behave consistently across users --- ## Real-World Considerations - High-security environments may: - Restrict external access completely - Disable screen sharing for non-hosts - Host management should always be enforced - Policies should align with organisational data protection requirements - Integration with Google Calendar enables seamless scheduling and joining --- ## Summary This section demonstrates: - Centralised Google Meet policy configuration - Secure meeting access and moderation controls - Controlled external collaboration - Support for hybrid workplace communication These configurations ensure meetings are secure, manageable, and scalable.
